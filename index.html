Rather than creating a live online store, this prototype focuses on improving the customer experience by organizing products, supporting online browsing, encouraging in-store pickup, and strengthening the Farm Store's omnichannel strategy. The guiding question for this project is: > **How should CPP Farm Store present its products online in a way that supports customer experience and sales?** ::: callout-note ![Screenshot 1](gp2_qmd_images/screenshot-01.jpg) ::: ------------------------------------------------------------------------ # Purpose of the Mini-Store Our group selected **gift baskets** as the primary focus of the Shopify prototype because they combine existing Farm Store products into curated collections that are easy to browse online while encouraging customers to visit campus for pickup. The prototype supports an online-to-offline customer journey by allowing customers to discover products digitally before completing their purchase through convenient in-store pickup. ------------------------------------------------------------------------ # Target Customer The CPP Farm Store's gift basket shoppers fall into two equally important customer groups. The first group consists of **Cal Poly Pomona students, faculty, and staff** who are looking for convenient, locally sourced gifts for birthdays, holidays, appreciation gifts, or care packages. These customers value convenience, online browsing, and the ability to order without making a special trip to the Farm Store. The second group consists of **local community members** from Pomona, Diamond Bar, Walnut, and surrounding cities who are searching for thoughtful, locally produced alternatives to traditional grocery store gift baskets. They appreciate the Farm Store's connection to Cal Poly Pomona, local agriculture, and student learning. Both customer groups benefit from an online storefront that allows them to browse gift basket options, compare products, and place pickup orders before arriving on campus. ------------------------------------------------------------------------ # Homepage The homepage introduces visitors to the CPP Farm Store by highlighting featured gift baskets, seasonal promotions, and convenient navigation. Gift baskets combine existing Farm Store products into higher-value purchases that are easy to browse online while encouraging customers to visit campus for pickup. The Shopify storefront extends the Farm Store's reach beyond on-campus shoppers by allowing customers to discover, compare, and purchase curated gift baskets through a digital storefront. Offering in-store pickup creates a seamless connection between online browsing and the physical retail experience. This strategy supports key ecommerce principles discussed throughout the course, including reducing friction in the customer journey, increasing average order value through bundled products, and creating a more convenient omnichannel shopping experience while maintaining the Farm Store's identity as a student-operated, community-focused business. ------------------------------------------------------------------------ # Trust & Service Information ## Store Location **CPP Farm Store at Kellogg Ranch** 4102 South University Drive Pomona, CA 91768 ------------------------------------------------------------------------ ## Store Hours - Monday–Friday: 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM - Saturday: 10:00 AM – 4:00 PM - Sunday: Closed ------------------------------------------------------------------------ ## Store Pickup Free in-store pickup is available for all online orders. One of the largest barriers for both CPP students and local community members was the inability to easily discover products before visiting the Farm Store. The online storefront allows customers to browse available gift baskets, compare products, and place pickup orders before traveling to campus. The prototype also strengthens the connection between online and offline channels by integrating online browsing with in-store pickup. Customers can discover products through digital marketing, browse curated gift baskets on Shopify, and conveniently complete their purchase on campus. Finally, the prototype creates opportunities for future email marketing, seasonal promotions, social media campaigns, and holiday gift collections that encourage repeat purchases while supporting the Farm Store's mission of promoting local agriculture and student learning. ------------------------------------------------------------------------ # Conclusion The Shopify prototype demonstrates how the CPP Farm Store can improve its ecommerce presence through organized product pages, clear navigation, convenient store pickup, and an integrated omnichannel customer experience. By combining online browsing with the existing physical Farm Store, the prototype provides a scalable foundation for future digital marketing initiatives and strengthens engagement with both students and the surrounding community. # Appendix ## GitHub Pages > ## GitHub Repository >
